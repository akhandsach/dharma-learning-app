In Ancient Indian Astronogy or Hindu Astronomy, there are 27 ***nakshatras*** ("lunar mansions", Sanskrit: नक्षत्र, IAST: *Nakṣatra*), or sectors along the ecliptic. A list of them is first found in the *Vedanga Jyotisha*, a text dated to the final centuries BCE. The *Nakṣatra* system predates the influence of Hellenistic astronomy on Vedic tradition, which became prevalent from about the 2nd century CE. The following list gives the corresponding regions of sky.

## Padas (quarters)
